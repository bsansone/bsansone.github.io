What is Project Hero?

Project Hero is an idea that was created through a course I took at Illinois Institute of Technology called:

Interprofessional Projects Program (IPRO) - Big Data Science & the Urban Experience Challenge

The main objective of this course was to develop insights from large data sets that can inspire solutions to human needs in the urban environment. Although our project didn't necessarily use big data in the end, it did help us along the way with figuring out our idea and mission.

What is the problem?

A significant number of Chicago high school students from low income communities are not pursuing a college education because of cultural barriers that discourage the continuation of their educational journey after high school.

Why does this matter?

This affects every community as low enrollment percentages correlate to high crime rates. Said another way, it costs taxpayers billions of dollars every year in crime related expenses.

What was our mission?

Our mission as Project Hero, is to establish an internet based awareness campaign that provides a platform to connect Chicagoâ€™s youth with successful, college educated professionals within their communities. Through this online tool we hope to increase the number of individuals pursuing a higher education through the empowerment of high school students from everyday heroes within their communities.

Facts and Stats from our Research:

  • - A 5% increase in male high school graduation rates, produces a saving of $5 billion in crime related expenses
  • - Of the states with the 10 highest college enrollment rates, 9 had violent crime rates below the national average
  • - Of the states with the 10 lowest college enrollment rates, 5 had violent crime rates above the national average
    • Even capable students are not pursuing college education...
  • - 1/5 of high schoolers with an ACT score of 24+ do not enroll in a 4yr college the fall after graduationm
  • - Over a quarter of high school seniors with a GPA of 3.5+ do not enroll in a 4yr college the fall after graduation
    • Low income students are especially hindered...
  • - Students with economic need have only a 30% chance of graduating from college within four years
  • - Students from wealthy families graduate at more than twice the rate of students from poor families who share the same SAT scores
    • Out of 100 Chicago High School Students...
  • - 40% enroll in 4yr colleges the fall after graduation
  • - 24% earn a degree within 6 years of graduating
  • - 86% are from low-income families
